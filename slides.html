The Basics

So what actually is AI?

Artificial Intelligence is software that can understand language, generate text, create images, write code, and reason through problems — trained on vast amounts of human knowledge.

Think of it less like a robot and more like an extremely well-read assistant who has absorbed millions of books, articles, and conversations — and can discuss almost anything with you.

Getting Started

Tips for your first week with AI

The biggest mistake beginners make is being too polite and too vague. AI responds to specificity and directness — like a capable colleague who needs clear instructions.

✍️
Be specific. "Write a 3-paragraph bio for a UX designer who loves hiking" beats "write a bio."
🔄
Iterate. If you don't love the answer, say so. "Make it shorter" or "add more humor" works perfectly.
🎭
Give it a role. "Act as a marketing expert and review this email" gets smarter responses.
🤔
Ask it to explain itself. "Why did you suggest that?" is always a valid question.

Web Apps

Build in a browser.
No setup required.

Web apps run in a browser — on any phone, laptop, or tablet. Claude Code's Desktop App can build these with zero technical setup on your end.

You can even make them feel like a real app on your phone — add to your home screen, works offline — these are called Progressive Web Apps (PWAs). No App Store, no Xcode, no $99/year developer fee.

Native iOS Apps

The App Store path is a whole different adventure.

Native iOS apps — the kind that live in the App Store — require a separate set of tools that even Claude Code can't skip.

🖥 Xcode — Mac only
Apple's development tool. Required to build and compile iOS apps. Does not run on Windows.
🍎 You must own a Mac
No way around this. iOS development is locked to Apple hardware.
💳 Apple Developer Account — $99/year
Required to publish anything to the App Store.
⌨️ Terminal time — unavoidable
Even with Claude Code helping, you'll end up in the command line at some point.
